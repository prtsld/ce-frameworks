MATERIAL SPECIFICATIONS

SHEET S-003 • ASTM COMPLIANCE • STRUCTURAL MATERIALS

Understanding the properties, advantages, and limitations of traditional construction materials is essential for making informed selection decisions. Each material must meet applicable ASTM, ACI, or AISC standards.

CONCRETE

  • advantages: high compressive strength, fire resistance, moldability
  • limitations: low tensile strength, high carbon footprint
  • applications: foundations, beams, dams, bridges
  • standard: ASTM C39, ACI 318

COMPREHENSIVE MATERIAL DATA:

  • Compressive strength range: 2,500 - 15,000+ psi (17-103 MPa)
  • Modulus of elasticity: 3,000 - 5,000 ksi (20-35 GPa)
  • Density: 140-150 lb/ft³ (2,240-2,400 kg/m³)
  • Thermal expansion coefficient: 5.5 x 10⁻⁶ /°F (10 x 10⁻⁶ /°C)
  • Carbon footprint: 0.9 tons CO2 per ton of cement
  • Reinforcement required: minimum 0.18% of cross-sectional area

MIX DESIGN CONSIDERATIONS: Water-cement ratio (w/c) directly affects strength: lower w/c = higher strength but lower workability. Supplementary cementitious materials (fly ash, slag, silica fume) can reduce carbon footprint by 20-50% while improving durability.

Reference: ACI 301-16 Specifications for Structural Concrete

STEEL

  • advantages: high strength-to-weight ratio, 100 percent recyclable
  • limitations: susceptible to corrosion, fire vulnerability
  • applications: structural frames, reinforcement bars, bridges
  • standard: ASTM A36, AISC 360

COMPREHENSIVE MATERIAL DATA:

  • Yield strength: 36,000 - 100,000+ psi (248-690+ MPa)
  • Tensile strength: 58,000 - 120,000+ psi (400-828+ MPa)
  • Modulus of elasticity: 29,000 ksi (200 GPa)
  • Density: 490 lb/ft³ (7,850 kg/m³)
  • Thermal expansion: 6.5 x 10⁻⁶ /°F (11.7 x 10⁻⁶ /°C)
  • Recycled content: up to 95% post-consumer

GRADE SELECTION GUIDE:

  • A36: General structural applications (36 ksi yield)
  • A572 Gr. 50: High-strength applications (50 ksi yield)
  • A992: Wide-flange shapes for building construction (50 ksi yield)
  • A500: Hollow structural sections (HSS) - 42-46 ksi yield

CORROSION PROTECTION: Coatings (paint, galvanized, weathering steel) add 20-50+ years of service life. Weathering steel (ASTM A588) forms a protective patina in suitable environments.

Reference: AISC Steel Construction Manual, 15th Edition

TIMBER

  • advantages: renewable, carbon-storing, aesthetic
  • limitations: fire risk, moisture sensitivity
  • applications: residential, mass timber buildings, finishes
  • standard: NDS, ASTM D245

COMPREHENSIVE MATERIAL DATA:

  • Design values vary by species: 500-2,000+ psi bending (3.5-14 MPa)
  • Modulus of elasticity: 1.2-2.0 million psi (8.3-13.8 GPa)
  • Density: 25-45 lb/ft³ (400-720 kg/m³)
  • Carbon sequestration: 1 ton CO2 per 1.3 tons of timber
  • Moisture content at delivery: 19% maximum for dimensional lumber

MASS TIMBER PRODUCTS:

  • CLT (Cross-Laminated Timber): Panels up to 10 ft x 60 ft
  • Glulam (Glue-Laminated): Beams and columns up to 100+ ft spans
  • DLT (Dowel-Laminated): No glue, mechanical connections
  • LVL (Laminated Veneer Lumber): High-strength engineered product

FIRE PERFORMANCE: Mass timber forms a char layer (approximately 1.5 inches per hour of fire exposure) that protects the inner core. Heavy timber construction can achieve 1-2 hour fire ratings without additional coatings.

Reference: NDS 2018 - National Design Specification for Wood Construction
