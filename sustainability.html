SUSTAINABILITY

SHEET S-004 • ENVIRONMENTAL IMPACT • LEED COMPLIANCE

The construction industry is increasingly adopting materials that reduce environmental impact while maintaining or improving performance. Every material choice affects the planet's future and contributes to embodied carbon calculations.

RECYCLED MATERIALS

  • recycled concrete aggregate — crushed concrete as base material
  • recycled steel — indefinite recycling without property loss
  • reclaimed timber — salvaged wood from demolished structures

RECYCLED CONCRETE AGGREGATE (RCA):

  • Applications: base course, subbase, drainage layers, new concrete (up to 30% replacement)
  • Environmental benefit: Diverts 140 million tons of C&D waste annually from landfills
  • Performance: Similar to virgin aggregate for non-structural applications
  • Cost: 10-20% less than virgin aggregate

RECYCLED STEEL:

  • Recycling rate: 98% of structural steel is recycled
  • Energy savings: 75% less energy than virgin steel production
  • CO2 reduction: 1.8 tons CO2 saved per ton of recycled steel
  • No degradation: Steel can be recycled indefinitely without property loss

RECLAIMED TIMBER:

  • Sourcing: Deconstruction of old barns, factories, warehouses
  • Carbon benefit: Keeps stored carbon out of the atmosphere
  • Aesthetic value: Unique grain patterns, historical character
  • Cost premium: 20-50% higher than new timber for specialty applications

Reference: USGBC LEED v4.1 Materials and Resources Credit

BIO-BASED MATERIALS

  • bamboo — rapidly renewable, high strength-to-weight ratio
  • hempcrete — hemp fibers with lime for insulation
  • cork — sustainable harvest for insulation and flooring

BAMBOO:

  • Growth rate: Matures in 3-5 years (vs 30-50 years for hardwoods)
  • Tensile strength: 28,000 psi (193 MPa) - exceeds steel's strength-to-weight ratio
  • Carbon sequestration: 400 tons CO2 per hectare
  • Applications: Scaffolding, reinforcement, flooring, structural panels
  • Standard: ISO 22156 - Bamboo Structural Design

HEMPCRETE:

  • Density: 200-400 kg/m³ (very lightweight)
  • Thermal conductivity: 0.06-0.1 W/mK (excellent insulation)
  • Carbon negative: Sequesters 110 kg CO2 per m³
  • Applications: Non-load-bearing walls, insulation infill
  • Limitations: Not structural (requires timber or steel frame)

CORK:

  • Harvest method: Bark stripped every 9 years, tree continues living
  • Thermal conductivity: 0.04 W/mK (superior insulation)
  • Acoustic absorption: NRC 0.15-0.30
  • Applications: Flooring, wall panels, underlayment

Reference: ASTM D7341 - Practice for Bio-Based Materials Testing

LOW-CARBON ALTERNATIVES

  • geopolymer concrete — reduces CO2 by up to 80 percent
  • mass timber — replaces steel and concrete in mid-rise buildings
  • carbon-cured concrete — sequesters CO2 during curing

GEOPOLYMER CONCRETE:

  • Materials: Fly ash, slag, alkaline activators (no Portland cement)
  • CO2 reduction: 80-90% less than conventional concrete
  • Compressive strength: 4,000-10,000 psi (27-69 MPa)
  • Setting time: 15-60 minutes (much faster than OPC)
  • Standards: ASTM C1157 - Performance Specification for Hydraulic Cements

MASS TIMBER:

  • Height potential: Up to 25 stories (currently 18 stories built)
  • Construction speed: 25% faster than concrete/steel
  • Carbon benefit: Sequesters 1.5 tons CO2 per ton of timber
  • Fire rating: 1-2 hours with char layer protection
  • Major projects: Mjostarnet (85m), Sara Cultural Centre (75m)

CARBON-CURED CONCRETE (CO2-Cured):

  • Process: Injects captured CO2 during concrete mixing
  • CO2 sequestration: 30-50 kg per m³ of concrete
  • Strength enhancement: 10-20% higher early strength
  • Technology providers: CarbonCure, Solidia, CarbonBuilt
  • LEED contribution: Up to 5 points for carbon sequestration

Reference: Carbon Leadership Forum - Embodied Carbon Benchmark Study
