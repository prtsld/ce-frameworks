BEST PRACTICES

SHEET S-005 • QUALITY ASSURANCE • STANDARDS COMPLIANCE

Implementing systematic approaches to material selection ensures consistent, high-quality outcomes across all projects. These practices form the foundation of responsible engineering and quality assurance protocols.

TESTING AND STANDARDS

  • ASTM international — standards for testing and specifications
  • AASHTO — transportation material standards
  • field testing — on-site verification of delivered materials
  • third-party certification — independent quality assurance

ASTM KEY STANDARDS FOR CIVIL ENGINEERING:

  • C39: Compressive Strength of Cylindrical Concrete Specimens
  • C78: Flexural Strength of Concrete (Simple Beam with Third-Point Loading)
  • A370: Mechanical Testing of Steel Products (tension, bend, hardness)
  • D2487: Classification of Soils for Engineering Purposes
  • E119: Fire Tests of Building Construction and Materials

FIELD TESTING PROTOCOLS:

  • Concrete: Slump test (ASTM C143), air content (ASTM C231), temperature (ASTM C1064)
  • Steel: Tensile coupons, bend tests, hardness (Rockwell, Brinell)
  • Soil: Proctor density (ASTM D698), CBR (ASTM D1883), Atterberg limits
  • NDT: Ultrasonic testing, radiography, magnetic particle inspection

QUALITY CONTROL FREQUENCY:

  • Concrete: 1 set of cylinders per 100 cubic yards (min 5 sets per day)
  • Steel: 1 coupon per heat of steel (each 20 tons)
  • Welding: Visual inspection of 100% of welds; NDT for critical connections

Reference: ASTM International Annual Book of Standards (Volume 04.02)

LIFECYCLE ASSESSMENT

  • raw material extraction — mining, harvesting impacts
  • manufacturing — energy use, emissions during production
  • transportation — distance and mode to site
  • end-of-life — demolition, recycling, disposal

LCA STAGES (EN 15978 / ISO 14040):

  • A1-A3: Product Stage (raw material supply, transport, manufacturing)
  • A4-A5: Construction Process Stage (transport to site, construction/installation)
  • B1-B7: Use Stage (use, maintenance, repair, replacement, operational energy/water)
  • C1-C4: End-of-Life Stage (deconstruction, transport, waste processing, disposal)
  • D: Benefits and loads beyond the system boundary (reuse, recovery, recycling)

SOFTWARE TOOLS FOR LCA:

  • Tally: Revit plugin for whole-building LCA
  • Athena Impact Estimator: Free, North American data
  • One Click LCA: Cloud-based, international databases
  • EC3 (Embodied Carbon in Construction Calculator): Free, building materials focus

TYPICAL CARBON INTENSITIES (kg CO2e per unit):

  • Concrete (25 MPa): 150-200 kg/m³
  • Structural steel: 1,500-2,000 kg/ton
  • Timber: -500 to -800 kg/m³ (carbon negative when sustainably sourced)
  • Insulation (fiberglass): 20-40 kg/m³

Reference: ISO 14044 - Environmental Management / Life Cycle Assessment

STAKEHOLDER CONSIDERATIONS

  • client requirements — budget, aesthetics, project goals
  • contractor input — constructability, local expertise
  • community impact — local sourcing, neighborhood character
  • regulatory requirements — building codes, environmental regulations

STAKEHOLDER ENGAGEMENT MATRIX:

  • High Power, High Interest: Manage Closely (clients, regulators, investors)
  • High Power, Low Interest: Keep Satisfied (government agencies, utilities)
  • Low Power, High Interest: Keep Informed (neighbors, advocacy groups, future users)
  • Low Power, Low Interest: Monitor (general public, media)

COMMUNITY BENEFIT AGREEMENTS (CBAs):

  • Local hiring commitments (20-50% of workforce from local community)
  • Local material sourcing (minimum 30% within 100 miles)
  • Traffic management plans during construction
  • Noise and dust mitigation measures
  • Affordable housing contributions (if applicable)

REGULATORY FRAMEWORKS:

  • International Building Code (IBC) - Chapter 16: Structural Design
  • Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) - Accessibility requirements
  • National Environmental Policy Act (NEPA) - Environmental review
  • Local zoning ordinances - Height, setbacks, use restrictions

Reference: PMI - Stakeholder Management Framework

DOCUMENTATION AND TRACEABILITY

  • material specifications — clear documentation of required properties
  • certificates of compliance — verification from suppliers
  • test reports — results from quality control testing
  • as-built documentation — record of materials actually installed

REQUIRED DOCUMENTATION BY PROJECT PHASE:

  • Design Phase: Material specifications, basis of design reports, reference standards
  • Procurement Phase: Mill certificates, supplier qualifications, material test reports (MTRs)
  • Construction Phase: Delivery tickets, batch plant reports, field test results, inspection logs
  • Closeout Phase: As-built drawings, warranty documents, O&M manuals, BIM models

TRACEABILITY REQUIREMENTS:

  • Concrete: Batch plant ticket includes mix design ID, date, quantity, delivery time
  • Steel: Heat number traceable to mill certificate (stamped on each piece)
  • Timber: Grading stamp (species, grade, moisture content, certification agency)
  • Welding: Welder qualification records, procedure specifications, inspection reports

RETENTION PERIODS:

  • Construction records: Minimum 7 years after project completion
  • Critical safety documentation: Life of structure + 10 years
  • As-built drawings: Perpetual (maintained by building owner)

Reference: ISO 9001 - Quality Management Systems
